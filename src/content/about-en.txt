My journey into tech didn’t start with a formal computer science degree—it started with a screwdriver and a healthy dose of curiosity. As a kid, I was constantly taking gadgets apart just to figure out how they worked (giving my parents a few minor heart attacks along the way). By the time I got my first phone, the Samsung Galaxy S, I was already hooked on tweaking it, pushing its limits by flashing custom software just to see what was possible.

For a while, I tried to steer that problem-solving energy into traditional paths. I explored economics and sales because it seemed practical, and later dove into industrial and electrical engineering, thinking hardware was my true calling. I even took a leap into self-employment as a sales representative. It was an incredible, fast-paced chapter where I learned how business works from the ground up, but something always felt missing. Every step of the way, I found myself looking for the tech angle.

Eventually, the pieces clicked: it wasn't the hardware or the sales goals driving me—it was the software. It was the thrill of building things that actually work.

That realization led me to fully commit to software and AI engineering at Masterschool, and for the first time, I feel like I am exactly where I’m supposed to be. I am incredibly passionate about the frontier of AI and its massive potential to genuinely better our lives. Today, I don’t just want to code; I want to build intelligent, impactful tools. I'm looking to bring this blend of real-world business grit, engineering curiosity, and pure technical passion to a team that’s building the future.
